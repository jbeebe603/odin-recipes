Katsu Sauce

Description

Yields 1 cup (4 oz) of sauce

Katsu sauce also known as "Tonkatsu sauce" is a Japanese Worcestershire type sauce. It is similar to "Brown Sauce" a product popular in the UK. It is not uncommon for different brands to include fish sauce, tomatoes, apples, lemon juice and various other fruits and ingredients common to Japanese cuisine. Tonkatsu was first made in 1948 by the Oliver Sauce company of Hyogo prefecture. The composition of the recipe often reflects cultural tastes, with the American Kikkoman Tonkatsu sauce being made primarily with applesauce, and Bull-dog brand in Japan leaning towards more umame flavors like malt vinegar and yeast.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup ketchup
  • 2 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 Tbsp Mirin
  • 1 1/2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp fresh grated ginger
  • 1 garlic, minced

Recipe

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Stir together in a large bowl and mix thouroughly.
  3. Let rest at least 30 minutes, but best if rested at least overnight.
  4. Can be used as a wing sauce as well, simply simmer in a pan and then pour over wings before tossing.

Tare Sauce

Description

Yields 1 cup (4oz) of sauce

Tare is a general term in Japanese for dipping sauces often used in grilling. It can also be mixed into broth when making soup to add complex flavors, or used as a braising liquid such as with Chashu. Tare is best described as a thickened, sweetened soy sauce for grilling. No two tare recipes are the same from chef to chef, with many adding extra flavors like dashi and ponzu, depending on their cooking and tastes. This will be a very basic Tare I use for grilled skewers (yakitori, gyutori), feel free to experiment and flavors that suit your taste or dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • 1 cup mirin
  • 1/2 cup sake
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons sherry vinegar
  • 6 cloves garlic, smashed and peeled
  • 6 scallions, roughly chopped
  • 2 inches of ginger, peeled and roughly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons whole black peppercorn

Recipe

  1. Combine ingredients in a medium saucepan, and whisk together over medium heat.
  2. Bring mixture to a boil before reducing to a simmer, cook until the sauce has reduced enough to coat the back of a spoon. (15-20 minutes)
  3. Once thickened make sure to strain and cool.
  4. Great for grilling meats and vegetables as well as using as a dipping sauce.
